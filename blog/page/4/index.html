From Wordpress to Tumblr

Jun 18th, 2011

Thanks to the script below for enabling me transfer my Wordpress posts to my newly created Tumblr almost painless. The script transferred most of stuff including title, tags, and categories correctly. Couple things are worth noting:

  • All Wordpress posts lost paragram formatting during the transfer so I had to go through each post to fix it. (This was the time I felt glad that I did not have many posts)
  • Table Contentin posts are gone and I cannot quickly figure out how to create table content in a Tumblr post. Thus I decided to take a screenshot and inserted it as an image.
  • Tumblr Theme installation process is very confusing, maybe just because I am a newbie. Every time I clicked install on a theme, it goes to my blog customize page, but it still displays those sample contents with my blog name; I clicked save+close several times, it did not work and I had to specifically click save to make it work. Is save+close supposed to be a superset of save?
  • The content editor for post is very narrow in Chrome (v12.0) and I cannot resize it. Is it a feature because Tumblr is a microblogging platform?

mwunsch:

Here’s a little command-line tool for importing WordPress posts to Tumblr. It works okay. Download it, chmod 755, and run it like this:

$: ./wp2tumblr wordpress.xml

Where wordpress.xml is a WordPress XML export. You can pass an optional --group parameter if you want to publish to a blog other than your main.

Some caveats:

  • All posts are regular posts.
  • Some post meta information is lost in the ether.
  • Comments are lost.
  • Anything not set to published will be saved as a Tumblr draft.

Update: I’ve updated the script to throttle requests to try to stay within the rate limit on api/write.

If there are less than 60 posts to import, this script uses Weary’s batch method for asynchronous requests. Each request is performed in its own thread (or your Ruby VM of choice’s approximation of a Thread). In other words, the script is really fast if you have less than 60 posts, but if not, will take 1 second per post to make sure you don’t exceed the rate limit.

Update: The good folks at Tumblr upped the rate limit on api/write from 10 to 60. Thanks Marco!

Tomcat Character Encoding Configuration for UTF-8

May 14th, 2010

Tomcat is still a very popular servlet container, but the default character encoding for Tomcat is ISO-8859-1. This causes problem if you want your website to be international because you will need to UTF-8 encoding. There is an instruction on Tomcat wiki talking about how to set character encoding. I just want to add some extra points.

  • If you are using Spring framework, you can use the character filter provided by Spring.

 <!--
    - This is useful because current browsers typically do not
    - set a character encoding even if specified in the HTML page or form
 -->
 <filter>
    <filter-name>encodingFilter</filter-name>
    <filter-class>org.springframework.web.filter.CharacterEncodingFilter</filter-class>
    <init-param>
        <param-name>encoding</param-name>
        <param-value>UTF-8</param-value>
    </init-param>
    <init-param>
        <param-name>forceEncoding</param-name>
        <param-value>true</param-value>
    </init-param>
 </filter>

 <filter-mapping>
    <filter-name>encodingFilter</filter-name>
    <url-pattern>/*</url-pattern>
 </filter-mapping>

    • Make sure that the character filter is the FIRST in your filter chain. This is actually very important since filter chain does have order.

    SNS in China

    Jul 11th, 2009

    Today I read an article from a Chinese tech site, it talks about the development of SNS industry in China. As we all know, after MySpace and Facebook took off in US, there are tons of copycats in China such as XiaoNei.com, KaiXin.com, KaiXin001.com and etc. There are a lot of competition between these early adopters, and you can even tell from their names: KaiXin.com and KaiXin001.com belong to two different companies and one of them is suing the other one. There are a lot of new comers, for example, Sohu.com is launcing its own SNS site. There are a lot of problems within this industry in China. Although they copy the idea pretty fast, even the look and feel, they do not have a good technical architecture and a good business model.

    Architecture wise, they have similar platform concept as Facebook and 3rd party developers can develope applications on that. But it does not provide a very good support for 3rd party applications developers and it leads to very few applications on the platform compared to thousands on Facebook.

    Business model wise, they position themselves as SNS, but what they are doing are off the track of real Social Network concept. They use webpage games to attract users which is fine. But most webpage games provide things like gambling, stealing and etc. which are not positive. Some of them are trying to shift their business model to Twitter model.

    Overall I think there is still a long way for SNS in China. Even in US, Facebook and MySpace are trying to find a good way to grow their business in a healthy way. SNS in China is trying to find a localized business model for China, and hopefully they can find a healthy way.

    Accessibility and You

    Jul 6th, 2009

    Today I went into an interesting talk by Chris Heilmann: Accessibilty and You. Before I got into this talk, accessibilty means to provide better user interface for people with disability. Apparently I am wrong. In this talk, Chris mentioned: “The fastest growing segment of the U.S. population is the 65 and over group.” Some statistics from facebook has a similar indication of the market shift. Apple, a company providing great user experiences, just released a good feature with their newest iPhone 3GS. I guess there is no best user interface, only better one =).
